Overview

The Bureau of Infrastructure and Transport Research Economics (BITRE) provides independent research, analysis and statistics on infrastructure, transport and related issues. Our work supports evidence-based policy development within the Australian Government.

BITRE produces a wide range of publications including statistical reports, research reports, information sheets and working papers covering transport modes, infrastructure, regional development and related areas.

Our publications are publicly available through the Resource library and are used by government departments, industry bodies, researchers and the broader community.

What we do

We collect, analyse and publish data and research on Australia's transport system and infrastructure.

Key activities

  • Collecting and publishing transport statistics
  • Analysing infrastructure investment and performance
  • Researching transport safety and regulation
  • Monitoring freight supply chains and logistics
    • Road freight movements
    • Rail freight volumes
    • Coastal shipping activity
  • Assessing regional connectivity and access

Publication schedule

Our reports follow a regular release cycle:

  1. Monthly statistical bulletins (aviation, maritime)
  2. Quarterly freight and logistics updates
  3. Annual yearbook and statistical summary
    1. Transport infrastructure yearbook
    2. Regional connectivity report
  4. Ad-hoc research reports and working papers

Data availability

Most datasets are published in both PDF and machine-readable formats (XLSX, CSV). Historical data is available on request.

Embargo policy

Pre-release access to statistical data may be provided to the Minister's office under strict embargo conditions.

Revision history

All corrections and revisions to published data are noted in the relevant publication's errata section.

Our research areas

BITRE's research spans a range of topics critical to Australia's economic and social wellbeing.

Good transport policy depends on good data. BITRE's research provides the evidence base that helps governments make informed decisions about infrastructure investment and regulatory reform.

BITRE Director, 2024 Annual Report

Aviation

Domestic and international aviation statistics, airline performance, airport activity and air service accessibility for regional communities.

Maritime

Port activity, coastal shipping, waterline reports on container handling productivity, and analysis of maritime freight movements.

Road transport

Vehicle fleet statistics, road safety analysis, traffic congestion measurement, and the economic costs of road crashes.
